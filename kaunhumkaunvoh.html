For centuries, India has earned its name as a nation that embodies unity in diversity. As citizens, we take great pride in this concept, yet it remains largely confined to theory. We all might have faced, seen, and been part of discrimination based on regional identity, language, and other factors. Regional discrimination has become so ingrained that it is often overlooked as a societal issue. It is extremely important to recognize that normalizing such behavior is even more detrimental than the problem itself.

People of different states in India engage in a competition to assert their dominance, often resorting to criticism and ridicule. For example, mockery directed at the Hindi pronunciations of South Indians, derogatory remarks about the appearance of people from the Northeast, and the use of terms such as “Chinese” to refer to them. People have even managed to strike two communities at once like in the case where people of Purvanchal are often referred to as “Bihari.” These actions are not only extremely poor attempts at humor but serve as a reflection of our societal biases.

The cumulative effect of these actions has resulted in disparate perceptions of these states. Let us consider the mental images we hold of these states: Jammu and Kashmir, Haryana, Punjab, Rajasthan, Bihar, Uttar Pradesh, Mizoram, Kerala, and Tamil Nadu, to name a few.

This exercise alone shows how bad the situation is. Imagine having none of these bad preconceptions instead of which we could have had a positive outlook. Regardless of whether we have visited them or interacted with their people, we unconsciously form judgments based on the image of their state biases. While this distinction may seem innocuous in casual conversation, it can lead to the unintended creation of negative stereotypes whose effects are underlined in red ink in all the pages of history that contain the dark acts of humanity. The consequences of these stereotypes can be profound and far-reaching as history and even the present world teaches us. A few examples include:-

  1. Auto drivers and laborers from Uttar Pradesh and Bihar frequently encounter harassment from locals of other states.
  2. Kashmiri Muslims are often stereotyped as extremists and pro-Pakistan.
  3. Housing societies in Mumbai prioritize Marathi residents over those from other states.
  4. Citizens of the Northeast faced discrimination during the COVID-19 pandemic.

Every single one of these issues, no matter how insignificant, erodes our unity. These divisions serve to hollow us out from within and create chaos. Historically, we know how the British exploited these divisions to enslave us through their infamous strategy of “divide and rule”, which we seem to be doing even in their absence.

Furthermore, social media platforms exacerbate these divisions. While intended to facilitate interaction, some of them have become platforms for the dissemination of false or misleading information, spreading hate. This further reinforces existing biases.

Technological advancements have outpaced us in our ability to adapt. It appears that technology has been imposed upon us without adequate time to learn its proper usage. Social media algorithms prioritize content consistent with users’ beliefs, leading to the reinforcementation of regional biases. Consequently, individuals are limited to information that reinforces negative attitudes towards certain areas, contributing to social division. Jokes and memes about specific fields generalize preconceived notions, resulting in individuals in certain regions becoming targets of derogatory comments or hate speech.

Misinformation about conflicts between regions on social media can escalate tensions and lead to real-world violence starting a very dangerous, exponentially growing loop. Instead of addressing violence, political groups exploit these divisions for their gain. Such actions foster enmity and resentment among individuals from different regions.

Discrimination arises from a complex interplay of historical, social, psychological, and economic factors. Social traditions, language, and flawed education perpetuate discrimination, while social trends reinforce it. Discrimination intensifies when groups compete for economic or social benefits. Historical conflicts and wars can also create divisions and prejudices between communities.

Psychologically rooted regional discrimination often originates from binary thinking and tribalism, which categorize the world into simplistic sections of “us versus them.” These mental constructs foster divisions among individuals, leading to prejudice, exclusion, and even hostility towards diverse regions. Tribalism entails prioritizing one’s group over outsiders. At the regional level, this manifests as regional biases where preferring individuals from one’s own state or city for job opportunities, admissions, or contracts and political polarization where politicians exploit regional identity to garner votes, aggravating community divisions.

We all are well too familiar with the challenges even a student faces moving to an unfamiliar city. Instilled with the biases we are instructed to seek acquaintance from only people of the same region and isolate ourselves from others. The difficulty of integrating with unfamiliar individuals in a new place compounded by differences can severely impact their self-confidence, leading to a sense of inadequacy.

Empathizing with another person’s pain can motivate individuals to confront discrimination. Therefore, it is crucial that we cultivate understanding and engage in thoughtful dialogue to ensure that our words do not cause harm, intentionally or unintentionally.

To address this issue, we must adopt a perspective that empathizes with others’ experiences. Recognizing that every individual possesses a narrative of their struggles, achievements, and identity is essential. We should critically examine our perceptions, identify their origins, and challenge prejudices that underpin them. By adopting a sympathetic mindset, we can effectively avoid discrimination.

Community-wide, we can propagate information about the detrimental effects of regional discrimination and the advantages of inclusivity through various channels. Additionally, we can establish safe platforms where individuals can engage in discussions about regional challenges and celebrate cultural diversity.

By addressing regional discrimination through these two approaches, we can foster individual development and collective action, ultimately contributing to a more compassionate society in India.
